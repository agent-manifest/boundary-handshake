**Boundary Handshake Agent — Agent Manifest Project — Draft v0.1**

Five quotable formulations from the manifesto, with notes on function and use.

---

## 01 — Core Principle

> Agents should not coordinate actions before declaring the boundaries under which they are willing to interact.

**Function:** The central normative claim. Self-contained — functions without surrounding context.

**Use:** Papers, repository READMEs, talks, social distribution, spec references.

---

## 02 — Problem Statement

> Agents can communicate, but they rarely declare the boundaries governing that communication.

**Function:** Describes the structural gap in one line. Opens arguments before introducing the solution.

**Use:** Paper introductions, talk openings, blog posts, position papers.

---

## 03 — Artifact Definition

> The Interaction Manifest is a structured declaration describing the conditions under which two agents agree to interact.

**Function:** Formal definition of the new artifact. Suitable for academic attribution and specification references.

**Use:** Spec citations, academic references, glossaries, technical documentation.

---

## 04 — Conceptual Thesis

> The terms of interaction are as important as the mechanics of interaction.

**Function:** Repositions the conversation away from protocols and tooling toward governance conditions.

**Use:** Essays, position papers, comparative analyses, conference presentations.

---

## 05 — Closing Statement

> The future agent ecosystem will not be defined only by what agents can do. It will be defined by how clearly they declare the limits under which they are willing to do it.

**Function:** Philosophical register. Strongest as a final beat after a full argument.

**Use:** Talk closings, paper conclusions, long-form posts.
